More about me.







My name is Genji Noguchi, and ...

I'm a New York native, Entrepreneur from Learning Curve Startup Accelerator, and hacker studying Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at the University of California at Berkeley.

My first real experience with engineering and technology was in my sophomore year, in which I took my first computer science course at Stuyvesant High School. It was also the year in which I joined the Science Olympiad Robot Arm team (more), where I got my first experience with building things, circuits, and tying it all together with software on the Arduino.

My love for technology and building things only grew from there. I went to my first hackathon in January of 2014, and have gone to 10 hackathons since, going as far as Canada for Hack the North. I got my first Android phone, an LG Optimus G Pro, and tinkered the crap out of it (nearly bricked it so many times). I led the team competing for Mission Possible, a Science Olympiad event about building a Rube Goldberg machine. Now, I'm on a team building an Android app to help connect people together, under Mobile Developers of Berkeley.

But, in order to build even cooler things, you need to get more knowledge to do so. Thus birthed my love for learning about the unknown. I spend a lot of my free time keeping myself updated with the latest tech news, watching and reading tutorials of concepts that I want to explore, and building projects to solidify my understanding.

But, as much as I like to take learning into my own hands, there's no denying the sheer amount of knowledge I've acquired from just one semester at UC Berkeley. Just imagine: Surface integrals, Stoke's theorem, a Scheme interpreter in Python, solving systems of equations with linear algebra, and even implementing LTE using signal processing, all in four short months!



Technology-related things aside, I'm an avid photographer, tennis player, and flautist. I also enjoy ultimate frisbee, billiards, and bombing hills on my cruiser.


TECHNOLOGIES

  • Programming Languages: Python, Java, C, C++, Scheme, SQL, JS, HTML/CSS, Android, Octave
  • Technologies: MarionetteJS/BackboneJS, EmberJS, AngularJS, Python Flask, NodeJS, NumPy, iPython

OTHER SKILLS
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Adobe Lightroom
  • ViM
  • Using a DSLR Camera
